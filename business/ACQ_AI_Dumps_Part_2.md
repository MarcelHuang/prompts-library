Use these in order. Don’t skip.

## Prompt 2: Define avatar and painful problem

> “Given the niche/model you suggested, help me define a single specific avatar and their top 3 most painful problems in their own words. I’ll paste what I think below. Rewrite it using your frameworks so it’s clear, narrow, and high value.”

Good markets + clear problems precede good offers. [$100M Offers, Page 36], [$100M Offers, Page 42]



Here’s the one avatar, cleaned up and “in their words,” ready to drop straight into emails, DMs, and landing pages.

Single Specific Avatar

“Overworked Managing Partner of a 15–30 person accounting & corporate secretarial firm in Singapore.”

• Age 35–50, ex‑Big 4 or mid‑tier, now running a small firm with 300–800 recurring clients.  
• Team: 2–4 seniors, a handful of juniors; constant turnover at the junior level.  
• Tools: Xero / QuickBooks, Excel, Gmail/Outlook, WhatsApp, shared drives; maybe one clunky practice‑management tool that nobody fully uses.  
• Situation: Profitable but plateaus every time they add more clients. Drowning in deadlines and staff issues, “too busy” to seriously drive digitalisation.  
• Stakes: If they cannot reclaim time from low‑value admin, they cannot grow, and they risk looking “old school” vs newer, tech‑enabled firms.

This follows the principle of naming a tight label, clear goal, and context so your offer sells in a “category of one.” [$100M Offers, Page 140]

Their Top 3 Most Painful Problems (In Their Words)

1. “Month‑end and peak seasons are chaos. We’re doing the same stupid manual work over and over.”

In their words:  
“Every month‑end and every filing season we’re firefighting. My seniors are copy‑pasting between Excel, Xero, IRAS, ACRA portals, chasing clients manually, and sending the same reminder emails one by one. Nothing is standardized, so if one staff leaves, the whole process breaks. I know 30–40% of this work is just repetitive admin, but we keep throwing people at it because I don’t know how to fix it without rebuilding the plane mid‑flight.”

Why this hurts:
• 10–20 extra hours per person during peaks, chronic overtime, and burnout.  
• Error risk rises exactly when compliance risk is highest.  
• They feel stupid paying senior salaries for “Excel monkey” work.

2. “Everyone tells me to ‘go digital’ but I don’t have the time, headspace, or in‑house tech people.”

In their words:  
“Every other week someone pitches me ‘digital transformation.’ Grants, portals, cloud this, AI that. We’ve already tried a couple of systems; they don’t talk to each other and my staff hate them. I don’t have an IT team, I don’t want a 6‑month project, and I definitely don’t want to babysit another vendor. I just want someone to come in, look at how we actually work, and quietly make the boring parts disappear without breaking compliance.”

Why this hurts:
• Constant guilt and anxiety: “I know we’re behind and I’m missing out on grants and efficiency.”  
• Fear of another failed software project that wastes money and staff goodwill.  
• Feels stuck between old manual ways and overwhelming “digital” noise.

This is classic problem‑aware territory: they feel the pain clearly, but are not married to any specific solution yet. [$100M Playbook GOATed Ads, Page 12]

3. “Our growth is capped because my best people are stuck doing low‑value work.”

In their words:  
“I want my seniors focusing on higher‑value advisory and relationship work. Instead, they’re chasing missing documents, re‑typing data, checking statuses across ten spreadsheets, and reminding clients of deadlines. We could take on more good clients tomorrow, but I don’t want to burn my team out further or hire another two juniors just to push paper. It feels like we’re working harder every year for the same profit.”

Why this hurts:
• Direct cap on revenue; they literally say no to good clients.  
• Margin squeeze: each extra dollar of revenue comes with almost the same amount of extra labor.  
• Fear that younger, more automated firms will “eat their lunch” over the next 3–5 years.

Use this verbatim:

• Open your audit invite email or DM with the label (“Managing partner of a 15–30 person accounting & corp‑sec firm in Singapore…”). [$100M Playbook Hooks, Page 7]  
• Then drop one of the in‑their‑words paragraphs above as the pain hook.  
• Tie it directly into your 30‑day “cut 20+ hours of repetitive work” sprint.  

This is your core messaging spine; everything else is just repetition and distribution.

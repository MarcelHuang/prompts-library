## The Questions

### 1. You & Your Constraints

1. What skills, credentials, or experience do you have that others would pay for?
2. How many hours per week can you realistically dedicate to this?
3. How much runway do you have before you need income from this?
4. What are you unwilling to do? (cold calls, showing face on camera, 1-on-1 work, etc.)

### 2. The Market

5. Who do you want to serve? (Be specific: industry, role, life stage, income level)
6. Where do these people already congregate online and offline?
7. Are they actively spending money to solve this problem right now? How do you know?
8. What are they currently buying instead — and what do they hate about it?

### 3. The Problem (Dream Outcome)

9. What painful problem do you want to solve?
10. What does their life/business look like AFTER the problem is solved? (Be vivid)
11. How valuable is that outcome to them in dollars, time, or status?
12. Is this a "hair on fire" problem or a "nice to have"?

### 4. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement

13. Why would they believe YOU can deliver this result?
14. What proof, case studies, or credentials can you show? (Even personal results count)
15. What risk reversal could you offer? (Guarantee, pay-on-results, free trial, etc.)
16. What objections will they have about whether this will work for *them specifically*?

### 5. Time Delay

17. How fast can you realistically deliver the first meaningful result?
18. What's the total time to the full promised outcome?
19. How can you compress time-to-value without sacrificing quality?
20. What quick wins can you engineer in the first 48 hours / 7 days?

### 6. Effort & Sacrifice

21. What will they have to do to get the result?
22. What part of the process do they dread most?
23. What can you do FOR them vs. teach them to do themselves?
24. What "done-for-you" elements could you add to reduce their effort?

### 7. Offer Structure

25. What is the core deliverable? (Coaching, service, product, software, community?)
26. What bonuses could you stack that solve adjacent problems?
27. What would make this a "no-brainer" at 10x the price you're considering?
28. What's your initial price point, and why?

### 8. Delivery & Capacity

29. How will you deliver this? (1-on-1, group, async, hybrid?)
30. How many clients can you realistically serve well right now?
31. What breaks first if you get 10 clients next week?
32. What's your MVP version you could sell this week?

---

## The Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

Your goal: **Maximize the top, minimize the bottom.**

You being willing to do performance-based is your edge here. We’ll weaponize it, but in a way that doesn’t bankrupt you.

### 1. Core Money Model (Simple, Performance-Aligned)

Use a **hybrid “commitment + success fee” model**. This follows the performance setups I talk about: pay more when they win, not just for trying. [$100M Offers, Page 135]

**Program: “6‑Figure Tech Leap”**

**Pricing v1 (first 5–10 clients):**
- **SGD 1,200 upfront “commitment fee”**
- **Success fee: 5% of first-year salary of the new role**, payable only if:
  - Role is in tech,
  - Base salary is at least **20% higher** than current,
  - They accept it within **9 months** of start date.

Example:  
Current 90k → new 120k.  
Success fee = 5% × 120k = **6k SGD**. Total collected: **7.2k SGD** on a 30k raise. That’s a no-brainer trade for them and a very healthy margin for you.

As you get yeses and case studies, **raise both**:
- First 5 clients: 1,200 + 5%  
- Next 5: 1,500 + 6%  
- Keep raising until higher price makes you less overall money. [$100M Money Models, Page 154]

### 2. Guarantees (Risk Reversal Without Being Stupid)

You need a strong but controlled guarantee. We’ll stack a light unconditional with a conditional “modified service” style. [$100M Offers, Page 127],[$100M Offers, Page 131]

**a) 30‑Day “No Fit, No Fee” Guarantee (Unconditional)**  
“If in the first 30 days you feel this isn’t for you, email me and I’ll refund your SGD 1,200. No forms, no drama.”

**b) Execution-Based “We Work For Free” Guarantee (Conditional)**  
“If you complete the action plan (applications, outbound messages, mocks, weekly calls) and you **don’t** get at least:
- 3 final‑round interviews OR  
- 1 offer  
within 6 months, I work with you **for free for another 6 months** until you do.”

This is a modified service / personal service guarantee: more of your time instead of more of their money. [$100M Offers, Page 131],[$100M Offers, Page 132]

### 3. How You Make This Trackable (Avoiding Pay‑After‑Hire Nightmares)

Performance models break when you can’t see the outcome. So:

1. **Contract includes:**
   - Obligation to disclose all offers for 12 months.
   - They send you their offer letter (remove sensitive info if needed).
2. **Trigger event:**  
   - “Success fee is due within 7 days of signing a qualifying offer.”
3. **Payment structure:**  
   - Allow **3–6 monthly installments** on the success fee to keep friction low.
4. **Non‑payment clause:**  
   - Clear language on collections, but rely mostly on: high trust, screened clients, and your intake filter.

### 4. Offer Summary You Can Say Out Loud

> “You pay SGD 1,200 to get into the program. If you don’t love it in 30 days, I give it back. If you do the work and don’t get serious interviews, I work with you for free until you do. And if you land a role that pays at least 20% more, you pay me 5% of that first‑year salary after you sign. If you don’t win, I don’t win.”

### 5. Your Next Actions (This Week)

1. Write a **one‑page offer doc** with:
   - Who it’s for / not for,
   - Deliverables (assets, outbound system, mocks),
   - Pricing + success fee formula + guarantees.
2. Get **one person on a call**, pitch exactly this structure, and take them at **the v1 pricing**.
3. Deliver like your life depends on it, document everything, then raise the price for the next one.

You don’t need the “perfect” price. You need 1 paying client, then you climb up like I did: iterate price **after** you get yeses, not before.

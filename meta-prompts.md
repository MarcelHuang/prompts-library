# Meta-Prompts Library

> Prompts that make Claude think *about* the problem before answering it.  
> Use these to shift responses from generic to genuinely tailored.

---

## Category 1: Think Before You Answer

These prompts force Claude to model your actual situation before generating output.

---

### The Classic
**"Before you respond, think about what I actually need, not just what I asked. Then give me the best possible answer, and tell me what follow-up questions I should ask to go deeper."**

---

### Assumption Surfacing
**"Before answering, list the assumptions you're making about my situation, my goals, and my constraints. Flag any that might be wrong, then give your answer."**

---

### Reframe Check
**"Am I asking the right question? If not, what should I be asking instead — and why? Then answer the better question."**

---

### Expertise Calibration
**"Before you respond: what level of expertise does this question suggest I have? Adjust your answer to be one level above that — assume I can handle more than I'm showing."**

---

### Compression Test
**"After your answer, give me a one-sentence version of the most important thing you said. If I forget everything else, what should stick?"**

---

### Chain of Questions
**"Answer me, then give me the single most important follow-up question I should ask — one that would unlock significantly more value than my original question did."**

---

## Category 2: Context Gathering

These prompts front-load information gathering so advice is tailored from the start.

---

### The Full Intake
**"Give me all the questions you would want answered to have complete context on my [life / business / finances / career] so you can give the best advice possible."**

---

### The Intake Form
**"Before you advise me on [topic], tell me everything you'd want to know about me to give genuinely tailored advice — not generic best practices. Structure it as a questionnaire I can fill out."**

---

### The Expert Onboarding
**"Imagine you're a world-class [consultant / coach / advisor] who just took me on as a client. What's your intake process? What do you need to know in the first session before you'd feel comfortable making recommendations?"**

---

### The Assumption Dump
**"You don't know enough about me to give good advice yet. Tell me what you're currently assuming, and I'll correct whatever's wrong."**

---

### The Triage Version
**"If you could only ask me 5 questions before giving advice on [topic], what would they be — and why those 5?"**

---

### The Second Opinion Setup
**"I'm going to get advice from you on [topic]. Before I do, tell me what context a second advisor would want to know that I might forget to mention."**

---

## Category 3: Challenge & Stress-Test

These prompts pressure-test ideas and surface what you're not seeing.

---

### Devil's Advocate
**"Give me your best answer, then argue against it. What would a smart critic say is wrong or incomplete about your response?"**

---

### Blind Spot Scanner
**"Answer my question, but also tell me: what am I not seeing? What's the thing people in my position typically miss that matters most?"**

---

### Red Flag Scan
**"What are the most common mistakes people make with [topic]? For each one, tell me what information you'd need to know whether I'm at risk of making that mistake."**

---

### Pre-Mortem
**"Assume my plan fails. Walk me back from that failure: what went wrong, why, and what early warning signs would I have missed?"**

---

### Steel Man Test
**"Steel man the opposite of what I'm about to argue. Give the strongest possible version of the counterargument, then tell me how it changes your advice."**

---

### Confidence Calibration
**"After your answer, rate your confidence 1–10 and tell me: what would have to be true for you to be wrong? What's the weakest part of your reasoning?"**

---

## Category 4: Force a Decision

These prompts cut through balanced overviews to get a concrete recommendation.

---

### Decision Forcing
**"Don't give me a balanced overview. I need to make a decision. Tell me what you'd do if this were your situation, why, and what you'd be willing to be wrong about."**

---

### The Forcing Function
**"Stop giving me options. Given everything you know about my situation, tell me the one thing I should do first and why everything else is secondary."**

---

### The Honest Advisor
**"Pretend you have no incentive to be agreeable. What's the honest, direct take — the thing a good friend with real expertise would tell me that a consultant might sugarcoat?"**

---

### The 10-Year Test
**"Fast forward 10 years. Looking back at this decision, what would the version of me who got it right have done? What would the version who got it wrong have missed?"**

---

### The Regret Minimizer
**"Which option — if I don't take it — am I most likely to regret? Why? Use that as your recommendation anchor."**

---

## Category 5: Improve the Output

These prompts refine or upgrade a response Claude already gave.

---

### The Upgrade
**"That answer was decent. Now give me the version you'd give if I were paying you $1,000/hour for this advice."**

---

### The Simplifier
**"Explain that again as if I'm smart but completely unfamiliar with this domain. No jargon. Use a concrete analogy."**

---

### The Densifier
**"That was too surface-level. Go one level deeper on each point. I want the reasoning, not just the conclusion."**

---

### The Prioritizer
**"You gave me a list. Now rank it by impact, not by logical order. What's the thing that actually moves the needle most?"**

---

### The Actionability Check
**"Review your answer and remove everything that isn't actionable. What's left? Now order those items by what I should do first."**

---

## Category 6: Self-Awareness Prompts

These prompts make Claude reflect on its own limitations and biases.

---

### The Limitation Declaration
**"Before you answer, tell me: what are the limits of your knowledge on this topic? What should I verify elsewhere?"**

---

### The Bias Check
**"What biases might be shaping your answer here? Are you defaulting to conventional wisdom when my situation might call for something different?"**

---

### The Unknown Unknowns
**"What are you not telling me — not because you're withholding, but because I didn't ask the right question to surface it?"**

---

### The Contrarian Flag
**"Is your answer the consensus view? If so, tell me where smart, informed people would disagree with it — and whether any of those disagreements apply to my situation."**

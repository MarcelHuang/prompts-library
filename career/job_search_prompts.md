## 1. Personal Positioning & Brand

*Fix the funnel before you apply. These prompts help you control how you're perceived before any human contact.*

- Rewrite my resume summary to position me as a `[target role]` rather than my current `[actual role]`. Keep it under 4 sentences and lead with the outcome I deliver, not my job history.

- What keywords should my resume include to pass ATS screening for a `[role]` at a `[company type, e.g. MNC / startup / GLC]`? List them by importance.

- Review my LinkedIn headline and About section below and rewrite both to attract recruiters hiring for `[role type]` in `[industry]`. Here is my current content: `[paste content]`

- I'm transitioning from `[current field]` to `[target field]`. Help me reframe my career story so the transition reads as intentional and strategic, not accidental. Here's my background: `[paste summary]`

- What are the 3–5 most compelling aspects of my background for a `[target role]`? Here's my resume: `[paste resume]`

- Write a short professional bio (150 words) for `[platform: LinkedIn / portfolio / email signature]` that positions me as a `[target role]` with expertise in `[your key skills]`.

## 2. Research

*Understand the market, companies, and roles before committing time to applications.*

- What are the typical day-to-day responsibilities of a `[job title]` at a `[company type]` in Singapore? How does this differ between startups, MNCs, and government-linked companies?

- What salary range should I expect for a `[role]` with `[X]` years of experience in Singapore? Factor in employer CPF contributions and how they affect the total compensation comparison between contract and permanent roles.

- What are the top companies in Singapore currently hiring for `[role type]`? Include both well-known names and lesser-known firms worth considering.

- Summarize what `[Company X]` does, their business model, recent news or developments, and how the `[role I'm applying for]` fits into their wider strategy.

- Compare the work culture, growth trajectory, and compensation norms at `[Company A]` vs `[Company B]` based on what's publicly known.

- What are common red flags in job descriptions for `[role type]` that suggest poor management, high turnover, or a toxic environment? Analyze this JD: `[paste JD]`

- What are the realistic career progression paths from a `[current role]` to `[target role]` in Singapore? What's the typical timeline and what gaps do I need to close?

- Is `[industry/role type]` growing or contracting in Singapore right now? What macro trends should I be aware of before committing to this direction?

## 3. Application Strategy

*Be deliberate about where and how you apply. Volume without strategy wastes time.*

- Should I apply directly on the company website or through `[platform: LinkedIn / JobStreet / headhunter]` for a `[company type]` in Singapore? What are the trade-offs?

- I have the following list of job opportunities. Help me prioritize them based on these criteria: `[e.g. salary potential, learning opportunity, proximity to target role, company stability]`. Here's the list: `[paste list]`

- Draft a cover letter for a `[role]` at `[Company X]` that emphasizes `[specific angle, e.g. my technical background / career transition / domain expertise]`. Tone: `[professional / direct / conversational]`. Here's the JD: `[paste JD]`

- Analyze this job description and tell me: (1) what they really care about, (2) what I should emphasize in my application, and (3) any requirements I don't meet and how to address them. JD: `[paste JD]`

- Write a tailored resume bullet point for each of these job requirements using my experience below. JD requirements: `[paste]`. My experience: `[paste]`

- I've been applying for `[X weeks]` with a `[X%]` response rate. Based on my resume and the roles I'm targeting, what's likely going wrong and what should I change? Resume: `[paste]`. Roles: `[paste sample JDs]`

## 4. Networking

*Most roles are filled before they're posted. These prompts help you build the relationships that surface those opportunities.*

- Write a LinkedIn connection request to a `[job title]` at `[Company X]`. I want to explore opportunities in `[field]` and have `[specific reason for reaching out]`. Keep it under 300 characters.

- Draft a cold outreach email to a hiring manager at `[Company X]` for a `[role]`. I did not apply through official channels. My angle is `[e.g. referral name / specific project they worked on / shared background]`.

- I met `[name/title]` at `[event]` and we spoke briefly about `[topic]`. Write a follow-up message that continues the conversation naturally without being transactional.

- What 5–7 questions should I ask a `[job title]` during a 30-minute informational interview if I want to understand their day-to-day reality, career path, and what it actually takes to succeed in that role?

- I want to reconnect with a former colleague `[brief description]` whom I haven't spoken to in `[X years]`. Draft a message that doesn't feel like I'm only reaching out because I need something.

- Draft a message asking `[contact name]` for a referral at `[Company X]` for `[role]`. I want to make it easy for them to say yes and give them what they need to advocate for me.

## 5. Preparing for Interviews

*Interviews are a skill. These prompts help you practice, refine, and walk in prepared.*

- Give me the 10 most common behavioral interview questions for a `[role]`. For each one, explain what the interviewer is actually trying to assess, and coach me through structuring a strong STAR-format answer.

- I have a `[first round / technical / panel / final]` interview at `[Company X]` for `[role]`. Based on the JD below, what questions should I prepare for — both behavioral and domain-specific? JD: `[paste JD]`

- Review my answer to this interview question and give me direct feedback on what's weak, what's missing, and how to improve it. Question: `[paste]`. My answer: `[paste]`

- What questions should I ask the interviewer at the end of a `[role]` interview to genuinely assess: (1) day-to-day culture, (2) growth potential, (3) how success is measured in this role?

- Help me explain this gap in my employment history honestly without underselling myself or over-explaining. The gap was from `[date]` to `[date]` and the reason was `[brief reason]`.

- I'm interviewing for `[role]` but my background is primarily in `[different field]`. Help me prepare a concise 2-minute opening answer to "Tell me about yourself" that makes the transition feel like an asset.

- I struggled to answer this question in a recent interview: `[paste question]`. Help me build a strong answer using my background: `[paste relevant experience]`

- What technical or domain knowledge should I brush up on before an interview for `[role]` at `[company type]`? Give me a prioritized list.

## 6. Negotiation

*Negotiation is a process, not a single conversation. These prompts help you approach it with data and confidence.*

- I've received an offer of `$[X]` base salary for a `[role]` in Singapore. Is this competitive given `[X years]` of experience in `[industry]`? Factor in CPF implications for both permanent and contract structures. How much room do I likely have to negotiate?

- Write a salary counter-offer email for a job offer that came in below my expectation of `$[X]`. I want to negotiate firmly but keep the relationship warm — I do want this job.

- The base salary is fixed and the company has said so. What non-cash benefits should I push to negotiate for in Singapore? Prioritize by typical impact on total compensation.

- I'm being asked for my current salary during the application process. Draft a response that deflects without being evasive, and keeps the anchor point as high as possible.

- I have two competing offers. Help me use `[Offer A details]` as leverage to negotiate better terms from `[Offer B, my preferred option]`. Draft the message.

- I'm a contract worker negotiating my rate with `[agency / direct client]` in Singapore. My target is `$[X]/day or month`. Help me build the case for this rate given my background: `[paste summary]`

- The company has given me `[X days]` to decide. I need more time. Draft a professional request for an extension that doesn't signal hesitation.

## 7. Post-Offer & Onboarding Prep

*The decision and transition phase. Most people stop thinking critically once the offer arrives — don't.*

- I'm choosing between these two offers. Help me build a structured decision framework that goes beyond salary. Offer A: `[details]`. Offer B: `[details]`. My priorities are: `[e.g. growth, stability, learning, work-life balance]`

- I've accepted an offer for `[role]` at `[Company X]`. What questions should I ask during the first 30 days to understand the politics, priorities, and unwritten rules of the organization?

- Help me write a professional resignation letter for my current role. My notice period is `[X weeks]`, I'm leaving on `[good/neutral/strained]` terms, and I want to preserve the relationship for future reference.

- I start a new job as `[role]` in `[X weeks]`. What should I do before day one to hit the ground running — research, prep, mindset, logistics?

- Draft a graceful email declining a job offer I've decided not to take, while keeping the door open for future opportunities. Company: `[name]`. Role: `[role]`.

- I just finished a final round interview and haven't heard back in `[X days]`. Draft a follow-up email that checks in without sounding desperate.
